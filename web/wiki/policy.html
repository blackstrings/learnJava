Policies In A Nutshell

The purpose of this page here is to only brief through some basic policies. Please read the full policies as given by the team leader for more details.

Traveling

You will have to go on a Store Visit during your first few weeks of working at Menards. Therefore, it is important to understand about the traveling policies and transporation availability.

There are different types of vehicles ( class 40-54) that Menards use.

  • Vehicle Mileage Report: You have to report or keep track your mileage per each vehicle you drive
  • Fuel Report: Make sure to include the fleet number of the vehicle and the gallons purchased.

Terminology

  • DIVISION: A unit of the company, i.e., Agri-Buildings Division, Lumberyard Division, Corporate Division.
  • Unit Number: A number assigned to each company unit, i.e., 3011 is Old Mill Center Yard, 2041 is Eau Claire Agri.
  • Fleet Number: Number assigned to each company vehicle including trailers, forklifts, etc. Important: When reporting fleet number, include the letter designation if vehicle has one.
  • Outside Purchases: Purchases of fuel, oil, parts, labor, etc. from suppliers or vendors outside the Menard organization.
  • Week: For purposes of this procedure, week is defined as starting Sunday morning and ending Saturday night.

Computer Usage

All computer traffics are monitored and belongs to Menards.

  • Keep data info secure and safe at all times.
  • Data is crucial to Menards and can terminate your employment at Menards if not carefully protected or used.
  • Do not use the computer for anything non-related to your job (ie. Facebook, Youtube, Twitter, News, etc)
  • exception: if you watch a Youtube video relating to your work in that you're using the video to solve an issue, then it is acceptable.

Gambling

Simply no gambling at work.

Relationships

Team members can date each other but not with Team Leaders nor Managers as it may create conflicts. If a relationship should occur between Team Member and Manager/Team Lead, the relationship must stop or one will have to leave employment.

Phone

Clock out if you need to make a non-related-job phone call, regardless if you plan to use your own cell phone or the phone at your desk office.

You may however answer non-related-job phone calls without having to clock out if you make it quick. Should be no more than a minute or two.

All job related phone calls are acceptable and you do not have to clock out
